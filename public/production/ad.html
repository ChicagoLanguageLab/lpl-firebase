Describe pictures and test your memory!

Language Processing Laboratory, Department of Linguistics, University of Chicago

Attention

Please do not complete this experiment more than once. You will not be paid for multiple submissions. Upon clicking the study link, you will be checked against our database of workers who have completed this study and will be alerted if you have already participated.

Note: If you have paritipated in the HIT "Record a sentence!", you are unfortunately not eligible for this HIT, since part 1 of this HIT contains the same survey. (Clicking the first link should confirm this.)

Overview

This survey examines how native speakers of English use language. It consists of two parts. In the first part, you will see a sentence and listen to a recording of said sentence. Then, you will be presented with an image and asked to record a sentence describing it. In the second part, you will perform two tasks. First, you will see sets of four images and will be asked to describe one image from the set. Next, you will participate in a standard memory test involving simple math and strings of letters. The entire study will take approximately 40 minutes to complete.

System Requirements

Hardware

Please make sure that you have the following equipment ready before beginning this study:

  • A pair of headphones.
  • A microphone. Either a built-in microphone or external mic may be used.
Web Browser

This study works best in Chrome or Firefox. Browsers like Internet Explorer or Safari will not be able to load the voice recorder.

Participant Requirements

Warning

If you do not meet these requirements, we cannot use your data. Please do not accept this HIT if you are not eligible.

In order to participate in this study, you must meet the following criteria:

  • You are at least 18 years old.
  • You are native speaker of American English. For our purposes, you are a native speaker of American English if:
    • You were born in the United States.
    • You lived in the U.S. from birth through age 12.
    • The members of the household you grew up in spoke primarily English.
  • You have no history of history of hearing loss, language disorders, or communication disorders.
  • You have no history of substance dependence, stroke, mental retardation, traumatic brain injury (with loss of consciousness lasting for more than one hour), multiple sclerosis, Parkinson's disease, Alzheimer's disease, Huntington's disease, schizophrenia, bipolar, autism, or ADHD.
  • You are not currently experiencing major depression.

Error
